In the last house of the great city, a small child lived. Her name was Amudha.

Amudha found a palm-leaf manuscript. It had letters on it — but she did not know which letter to learn first.

"Mother, which letter should I learn first?" she asked.

Her mother smiled. "The letter 'A' (அ). It comes before all other letters."

Amudha wrote the letter 'அ'. At that moment, a soft light glowed from the palm leaf.

"Why the light?" Amudha asked.

Then a voice came to her ear:

> அகர முதல எழுத்தெல்லாம்
> ஆதி பகவன் முதற்றே உலகு
>
> *As A is the first of all letters, so the eternal God is first in the world.*

"Thiruvalluvar says this," her mother explained. "For letters, 'A' comes first. For the world, God comes first. Whenever any learning begins, the first act — the offering of gratitude — that beginning is the most beautiful of all."

From that day on, every time Amudha learned a new letter, she began with a small bow of reverence.

Because she had learned that **every beginning should hold a moment of reverence**.
