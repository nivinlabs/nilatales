On the slopes of a hill, a young boy was tending his goats. His name was Murugan.

One day, a heavy rain came. The goats bolted in fear. Murugan called after them — but no one listened.

"Oh, I can't do this!" he wept.

Just then, an old man came by. "Tears cannot stop the rain, my son. The rain will come — but offer your gratitude."

"Gratitude for the rain?" Murugan asked, surprised.

"Yes. It is the rain that makes the grass grow. It gives food to the goats. Without rain, there is no grass — and no goats."

Murugan understood. He looked up at the sky and said, "Thank you!"

The rain stopped. The goats came back. Fresh grass sprouted.

The old man spoke:

> ஈனார் எல்லாம் தலையாய் முன்னார்
> வான் புகழ் மேல் கிடந்து உலகு
>
> *He who dwells in heavenly praise is the head of all who learn. He who gives gratitude conquers all.*

"From that day on, every morning, Murugan looked up at the sky and said 'Thank you.'"

**Because gratitude is the summit of life** — that is heavenly praise.
