In a little village, a small moon lived in the sky. She had light — but she did not want to share it with anyone.

"This light is mine," she would say. "I alone will enjoy it."

One night, a small child came outside and looked up at the moon.

"Mother Moon, will you give me a little light? My path is dark," the child asked.

The moon hesitated. But she saw tears in the child's eyes.

She sent down a small ray of light. At once, the lane glowed.

The next day, another child came. "Mother Moon, will you give me some too?"

Now the moon understood — giving light had not diminished it. It had only grown.

From that night onward, the moon shared her light with everyone. The whole village glowed.

The moon was happier than she had ever been. Because she had learned that **true light is found in sharing**.
