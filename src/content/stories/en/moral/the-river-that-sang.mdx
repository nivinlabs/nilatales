At the mountain's peak, a tiny spring was born. It grew into a river. The river sang — it was always singing.

"Lilee, lilee," it murmured as it ran over the stones.

A great boulder stood in the way. "You cannot pass me!" the boulder declared.

The river did not stop. It flowed around the boulder. The song changed — "I'll flow around, I'll flow around!"

A forest appeared. The trees blocked the way. The river slipped between the trees. "I'll bend and weave, I'll bend and weave!" it sang.

A barren field lay ahead. The river brought water there. The field turned green. "I'll bring abundance, I'll bring abundance!" it sang.

At last, the sea appeared. The river merged with the ocean. But the song did not stop — it became the waves.

**Every obstacle made the river's song more beautiful** — because the obstacles themselves are the verses of the song.
