In a small village lived a farmer and his wife. They had a tiny baby. And in that same house lived another beloved creature — a mongoose. The mongoose had been raised in the home even before the baby was born, so everyone loved it as a member of their own family.

The mongoose was very gentle. When the baby lay in the cradle, it would sit quietly nearby. When the baby laughed, the mongoose would wag its tail with delight.

"Our mongoose is a fine guardian," the farmer would often say.

One afternoon, the farmer went out to the fields. The baby was sleeping in the cradle. The mother needed to fetch water from the well with a pot.

She spoke to the mongoose: "I'll be back soon. Keep an eye on our little one."

The mongoose lifted its head as if it understood. Once the mother stepped out, the house fell silent. The sun blazed outside. Inside, only the soft sound of the baby's breathing could be heard.

Just then, a black cobra slithered in through a small hole in the back wall. It glided slowly across the floor, heading toward the cradle. Its tongue flickered. Its eyes gleamed menacingly.

The mongoose leapt up at once. Every hair on its body stood on end with alertness.

"Don't come any closer!" it seemed to snarl.

The cobra raised its hood and hissed. The mongoose was small; the cobra was long and powerful. But the mongoose did not back down. It knew the sleeping baby in the cradle could not protect itself.

The cobra suddenly lunged forward. The mongoose dodged swiftly. The cobra struck again. The mongoose tried to seize the cobra by the neck. Dust rose from the floor. A pot rolled away. The baby stirred slightly — but was still asleep.

The mongoose got scratched on its face. Blood dripped down. Yet it did not let go. With one single thought — "No harm shall come to this child" — it fought on.

Finally, the mongoose lunged at exactly the right moment and bit down on the cobra's neck. The snake weakened and collapsed to the floor. After a little while, it lay motionless.

At that very moment, the mother returned home with the pot of water. The mongoose came running to the doorway. Its mouth and face were smeared with blood.

"Oh no!" the mother cried out. "Have you hurt my baby?!"

In her fear and anger, she raised the pot to throw it at the mongoose. But just then, from inside the house, she heard the soft, gentle laugh of her baby. She rushed inside.

The baby was perfectly safe in the cradle. Near the cradle lay the dead black cobra. On the floor were the clear signs of a fierce struggle.

Tears streamed down the mother's cheeks. With trembling hands, she scooped up the mongoose.

"Forgive me, my brave little one," she wept. "You saved my baby's life."

When the farmer came home that evening, he heard everything that had happened. He gently cleaned the mongoose's wounds and applied medicine. From that day on, the whole village talked about the mongoose's courage.

**True bravery is lovingly protecting those who cannot protect themselves.**
