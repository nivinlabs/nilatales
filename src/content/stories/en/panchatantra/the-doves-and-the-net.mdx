The first tantra of the Panchatantra is Mithrabheda — the losing of friendship. This is one of its famous inner tales.

Near a forest, a hunter spread a net to catch doves. A dove named Chitragriva came with his flock, searching for grain.

"Come here, there's grain here!" called one dove.

The moment they landed, the net fell upon them!

"Oh no, we're all caught!" the doves cried.

But Chitragriva stayed calm. He said:

"Friends, do not be afraid. We will all fly up at the same time. Let the net come with us. But we must fly as one."

All the doves flapped their wings at once. Together they rose into the air. The net rose with them!

The hunter stood below, watching in amazement. He could not pull the net down.

Eventually, Chitragriva found a mouse — who gnawed through the net and set them free.

**The lesson:** "Our unity is our strength. A single dove alone will fall, but when a flock flies, even the net is lifted."
