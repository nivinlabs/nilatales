In a forest, a cruel lion ruled. He commanded that every day, one animal must be sent to him as food.

"If not, I will destroy you all!"

The animals were terrified. Each day, one of them went.

One day, it was the turn of a small rabbit. The rabbit was not afraid. "I will devise a trick," he thought.

The rabbit arrived late. The lion was furious. "Why are you late?"

"Oh, Your Majesty! On the way, a new lion stopped me. 'I am the king of this forest,' he declared!"

The lion's anger blazed. "Where is he? Show me!"

The rabbit led him to an old well. "Look, Your Majesty — that lion is inside."

The lion peered into the well. He saw his own reflection. "Is that you?" he roared. The reflection roared back.

In a rage, the lion leapt into the well. He drowned.

The rabbit returned to the forest. "The forest is free!"

**The lesson:** Strength alone is not enough. **Wit is the true strength.**
